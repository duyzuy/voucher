1. KOREA
Entry conditions
  • Korean nationality;
  • Holder of ABTC/ APEC card;
  • Holder of Residence card/ Overseas Korean resident card;
  • Holder of a valid Korean visa (including K-ETA for those of eligible nationals, etc.)
  • Holder of A-1, A-2 visa (Diplomatic); F5, F6 (Korean spouses and children)
  • Residence card holders (Alien Registration Card),
  • Eligible long-term visa holder.
    * Short-term visas issued before April 5, 2020 will not be valid.
Before departure

1. Testing requirement

  • All passengers must provide certificate of a negative COVID-19 test result.  
  • Test method: NAATs (RT-PCR, LAMP, TMA, SDA, NEAR, etc.), the certificate must be issued within 48 hours (two days) before the departure date or a supervised negative COVID-19 RAT result must be issued within 24 hours prior to the departure date, Certificate must in English or Korean hard copy. Electronic certificates are not accepted. 
  • Exception for Covid test:
    • Children under 6 years of age;
    • Korean nationals entering Korea within 10 to 40 days of the date when they tested positive for COVID-19* (Responsible for providing positive test certificate themselves)
    • Foreign nationals on a long-term stay who are infected with COVID-19 in Korea entering Korea within 10 to 40 days of the date when they tested positive for COVID-19 (only applied to those holding at least one of the following documents: residence card, overseas Korean resident card, permanent resident card or ID card of diplomatic mission in Korea). 
    • ‘US Forces in Korea’ passengers who were infected with COVID-19 in Korea entering Korea within 10 to 40 days and has ‘Confirmation of Release Form Isolation of Covid-19 Patients’ issued by US Forces in Korea. (Also applied to those holding ‘US Forces’ ID card or which has ‘Verified’ A-3 mark in the passport).
    • Details here

2. Q-Code Application

  • Quarantine exemption program for passengers from Vietnam to Korea will be applied. This propram only for the passengers who completed Korean “Q-Code” information in the App before Korea Arrival. (Q-code is a system to make faster quarantine inspection and provide the conveniences overseas entrants require upon entry to the Republic of Korea. For detail please refer https://cov19ent.kdca.go.kr 
  • Q-Code completed: Exemption of Quarantine and not need to fill “Health Declaration” form and “Travel Record Declaration” form

3. Health Declaration

  • Passengers use Q-code system to input information for fast and convenient quarantine inspection after arrival at the Republic of Korea. Passengers who have completed with Q-code, will not need to fill “Health Declaration” form and “Travel Record Declaration” form. Detailed Q-Code system https://cov19ent.kdca.go.kr. 
  • Self-Check Mobile App(자가진단앱) is required for passengers who are exempted from quarantine. (http://ncov.mohw.go.kr/selfcheck/)
Quarantine regulations
  • All passengers entering into Korea will be exempted from quarantine, effective from 8 June 2022. Accordingly, issuance of ‘Quarantine Exemption Certificate’ will be suspended (It will maintain for funeral attendance or official business reasons, which replaces the COVID-19 negative certificate).
  • 07 Days of quarantine and need to fill “Health Declaration” form and “Travel Record Declaration” form If haven’t completed Q-Code yet.
  • Short-term visa holders (Short-stay foreigners under 90days) who have NOT completed vaccination must be in facility quarantine for 7 days at their own cost (120,000KRW/day). Passenger must complete the “Facility Quarantine Consent Form” and submit it to the quarantine officer on arrival.
  • Passengers are considered to have completed their immunizations when they meet one of the following requirements: Complete the 2nd dose within 14 to 180 days (for Janssen only need 1dose); Received a booster shot (3rd dose).
  • Passenger who is under 18 years old, have completed vaccination: 14 days following the second dosage of a vaccination (1st dose for Janssen) that has been approved in Korea/outside of Korea.
  • Detail of quarantine at “ICN AIRPORT QUARANTINE FLOW”

From 25JUL2022 (Korea Arrival Local time):

All Passengers who arrive/ enter Korea must take PCR test within 1 day (acceptable next day 24:00).

3. TAIWAN
PAX (Who are allowed to entry)
  • Taiwanese nationality.
  • Passengers with a "SPECIAL ENTRY PERMIT FOR COVID-19 OUTBREAK" visa with "CC", "CF", "DC", "ER", "FC", "FD", "FO", "FR", "FS", "HC", "HS", "OC", "OS", "SC", "SF", "TC" or "TS" stated on remarks.
  • Passengers with a "SPECIAL ENTRY PERMIT FOR COVID-19 OUTBREAK" visa with "X" stated on remarks issued after 21 October 2021.
  • Passengers with a MOFA ID.
  • Foreign nationals may enter Taiwan for business activities including business visits, investment, fulfillment of contractual obligations, and employment; applicants should apply for a special entry permit at an R.O.C. overseas representative office.
  • Passengers with a special visa “FL” stated on remarks is a labor working visa, the passenger’s name must be on the approved labor list provided by TPE airport labor office.
  • Effective on 25 July 2022, the entry restrictions for foreign nationals will be loosened; foreign nationals may apply for a special entry permit visa to visit Taiwan for the following purposes: volunteering, religious proselytization, studying religious doctrines, internships, international exchanges, and working holidays (youth exchanges).

    • Pax choose program C: Need to prepare proof of full vaccination
    • Others: Vaccinations not required.
Covid test requirement
  • Passengers must have a negative COVID-19 test result within 02 days which is based on specimen collection date to departure date.
  • Accepted tests are: LAMP, NAAT, PCR, RNA, RT-LAMP and RT-PCR.
  • From 00:00 on July 14, 2022 (incoming flight's scheduled arrival time in Taiwan), the following passengers will be exempt from providing a PCR test report which was taken within 02 days of flight schedule time:
    • R.O.C. nationals (Taiwan passport holders)
    • Alien Resident Certificate holders (Taiwan ARC holders), except for re-entry laborers
    • Passengers who transit and transfer at Taipei
    • The diplomats in Taiwan and their family members holding the visa with mark "DC" and "FO".
  • However, those who test positive for COVID-19 abroad must wait over 07 days from the specimen collection date to take a flight to Taiwan in order to maintain safety on the flight and epidemic prevention efforts in the community. Reference at here

After Arrival
  • Turn on mobile phone to receive an electronic health declaration receipt (if mobile phone is not roaming, passengers buy Sim card at airport)
  • Present Online health declaration form
  • Quarantine requirement:

Effective from 00:00 15 Jun, 2022, (based on the incoming flight's scheduled arrival Taiwan time) the quarantine period will be shortened:

1. Required quarantine period and quarantine location:

    • 3+4: The day of arrival counts as Day 0; a three-day home quarantine and four-day self-initiated epidemic prevention following the end of quarantine will be required.
    • An arrival should observe the rule of one person per residence in his or her quarantine location or stay in a quarantine hotel; in principle, the arrival should undergo the three-day quarantine and four-day self-initiated epidemic prevention in the same location.

2. Testing measures:

    • PCR testing: deep-throat saliva samples will be collected from arrivals for PCR testing at the airport on the day of arrival (Day 0).
    • At-home rapid tests: two rapid test kits will be given to arrivals aged 2 and older by workers at international airports upon arrival; arrivals can use the test kits when symptoms occur during quarantine and before they go out for the first time during the self-initiated epidemic prevention period.

3. Transportation from the place of arrival to the quarantine location:

    • Besides taking quarantine taxis, arrivals can get a ride from their friends, relatives, companies or organizations, and epidemic prevention measures for quarantine vehicles as well as other prevention protocols should be followed.

6. MALAYSIA

Procedure for inbound travellers
  • All passengers are no longer required complete the pre-departure form via the Traveller icon on MySejahtera application.
  • All Covid-19 Pre-departure tests (both RT-PCR and ART) are NO longer required.

Before boarding

Required documents:

  • Valid immigration documents (Passport, Visa, Stay Permit);  
  • A return ticket or a one-way ticket to continue the journey to another country;

All foreign travelers (foreign nationals) who will enter the territory of the Republic of Indonesia shall adjust the general travel requirements to be allowed to enter the territory of Indonesia, which includes COVID-19 vaccination certificate and the use of PeduliLindungi application.   

  • The vaccination card or certificate (physical or digital) must be written in English or bilingual.  
  • Foreign travelers are not required to show a negative RT-PCR test result in which samples were taken in the country or region of origin. 
  • Installed Peduli Lindungi app and registration before check-in at departure airport;
  • Completed e-CD Customs Declaration Form at https://bcngurahrai.beacukai.go.id/ecd/?h=beranda    

     

The provision for showing COVID-19 vaccination certificate (physical or digital) is excluded for all foreign travelers with the following categories:  

  • Foreign nationals who hold diplomatic or official visas related to official/state visits of their foreign officials at the ministerial level and above and foreign nationals who enter Indonesia under the Travel Corridor Arrangement scheme, according to the principle of reciprocity;  
  • Foreign nationals who intend to travel domestically in order to go outside the territory of Indonesia, as long as they do not leave the airport area during the transit process, provided that they (i) have been authorized by the Port Health Office and (ii) show the flight ticket schedule for direct transit from the city of departure to the international airport in Indonesia;  
  • Under 18 years of age;  
  • Have finished the COVID-19 quarantine and have been declared clear from COVID-19, required to bring a COVID-19 recovery certificate from a government hospital or Ministry of Health in the country/region of origin of departure;  
  • Have a special health condition or comorbid disease that prevents the foreign travelers from receiving the COVID-19 vaccination, as evidenced by a doctor's certificate from the government hospital of the country/region of origin of departure.  

From June 13th 2022, Health insurance with minimum coverage of 500,000 IDR, including COVID-19 financing is no longer required for all foreign travelers to Indonesia.

After arrival

Upon arrival at the entry points, foreign travelers must undergo a COVID-19 symptom check, including a body temperature check, with the following conditions:  

  • If there is no symptom of COVID-19 and body temperature is below 37.5 degrees Celsius: no need to undergo RT-PCR examination upon arrival.  
  • If there is a symptom of COVID-19 or body temperature above 37.5 degrees Celsius: must undergo RT-PCR examination upon arrival.

After leaving the airport, passengers wait for the on arrival RT-PCR test result in their hotel room or place of residency, which will appear in the Peduli Lindungi app. It is not allowed to leave the hotel room or place of residency before the RT-PCR test results show a negative result. If negative, you are free to continue with activities according to travel plans or tour packages.  

 

If you test positive, you may be moved to an isolation hotel or hospital at your own expense as mandated by the COVID task force.

Quarantine requirements
  • The provision regarding the period of isolation (quarantine) applies for all foreign travelers with the following categories:  
  • Quarantine is no longer required for those who have received the complete dose of vaccine at least 14 days before the departure time.   
  • Quarantine period of 5 x 24 hours is required for those who have not been vaccinated or received the complete dose of vaccine.  
  • Foreign travelers under the age of 18 (eighteen) years and/or require special protection can follow the quarantine period provisions imposed on their parents or caregivers.
